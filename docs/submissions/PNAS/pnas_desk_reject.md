Thank you for submitting your manuscript, titled "Gender in Teaching: Insights from Five Million Syllabi on Collaboration, Interdisciplinarity, and Reading Selections", to PNAS; the results of our assessment have led us to the decision to decline to consider it for publication at this time.

PNAS is a multidisciplinary journal that aims to publish high-impact research of general interest to the scientific community. Because we receive more than 18,000 submissions every year, incoming manuscripts undergo an initial evaluation by a member of the Editorial Board, who is also a member of the National Academy of Sciences, to determine whether the potential novelty, impact, and relevance in the broad scientific community merit further detailed technical review. In your case, our assessment is that your manuscript does not meet one or more of the principal aims of our journal and on this basis we expect that the likelihood that detailed review will lead to publication is low. This decision is necessarily subjective and does not reflect an evaluation of the technical quality of your work.

The Editorial Board member, however, suggests that your work may be of interest to PNAS Nexus, a gold open access journal from the National Academy of Sciences, with a focus on high-quality, innovative multi-, trans-, and interdisciplinary work across the biological, physical, and social sciences, particularly encompassing engineering and the health sciences.

If you would like to transfer your manuscript for consideration at PNAS Nexus, please follow the link below. You will not have to reformat your manuscript for consideration by PNAS Nexus.
