Dear Editors of PNAS Nexus,

We appreciate the opportunity to transfer our manuscript from PNAS to PNAS Nexus for further consideration. Following the thoughtful evaluation by the PNAS Editorial Board, we agree that PNAS Nexus’ focus on high-quality, innovative interdisciplinary research aligns well with the scope and aims of our study.

Our manuscript addresses an important and understudied aspect of gender dynamics in academia—specifically within teaching and course design. Despite growing gender diversity in academic faculty, we reveal a persistent underrepresentation of mixed-gender teaching teams across universities by analyzing five million syllabi spanning 1999 to 2019. Our findings show that instructors tend to co-teach with same-gender colleagues at more than twice the expected rate, a pattern not fully explained by institutional or disciplinary constraints, indicating gender-based bias. Furthermore, we demonstrate that mixed-gender teams design more interdisciplinary courses and assign more gender-diverse readings, while women tend to incorporate more novel materials. These insights extend the literature on gender bias into the realm of pedagogy, with important implications for understanding how collaboration patterns influence course content and student experience. We believe these findings can inform policies aimed at improving equity and pedagogical quality in higher education.

Given the interdisciplinary nature of our work, encompassing social sciences, education, and gender studies, we believe PNAS Nexus is an ideal venue to reach a broad and diverse scientific audience interested in multi- and transdisciplinary research.

We look forward to the consideration of our manuscript by the PNAS Nexus editorial team and thank you for your time and attention.

Sincerely,
